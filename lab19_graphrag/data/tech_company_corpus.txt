OpenAI was founded in 2015 by Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Elon Musk, and Wojciech Zaremba. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California. Sam Altman currently serves as CEO of OpenAI. OpenAI released GPT-4 in 2023, one of the most capable large language models available to the public. OpenAI also released ChatGPT in 2022, which became the fastest-growing consumer application in history.

Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin. The company is headquartered in Mountain View, California, at a campus known as the Googleplex. Sundar Pichai serves as CEO of Google. Google released the Bard AI assistant in 2023, later rebranded as Gemini. Google acquired DeepMind in 2014, bringing one of the world's leading AI research labs under its umbrella.

Microsoft was founded in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen. The company is headquartered in Redmond, Washington. Satya Nadella serves as CEO of Microsoft. Microsoft invested heavily in OpenAI starting in 2019, eventually committing over ten billion dollars to the partnership. Microsoft released Copilot, an AI assistant integrated into its Office 365 suite, in 2023. Microsoft acquired LinkedIn in 2016 and GitHub in 2018.

Apple was founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. The company is headquartered in Cupertino, California, at its Apple Park campus. Tim Cook serves as CEO of Apple. Apple released Apple Intelligence, its suite of on-device AI features, in 2024. Apple is a competitor of both Google and Microsoft in the personal computing and smartphone markets.

Meta Platforms was founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg. The company is headquartered in Menlo Park, California. Mark Zuckerberg serves as CEO of Meta. Meta released the Llama family of open-source large language models in 2023. Meta acquired Instagram in 2012 and WhatsApp in 2014. Meta also operates a large AI research division known as FAIR, the Fundamental AI Research lab.

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, and several former OpenAI researchers. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California. Dario Amodei serves as CEO of Anthropic. Anthropic released the Claude family of large language models, with Claude 3 Opus being released in 2024. Google invested in Anthropic in 2023, making it one of the major backers of the company. Amazon also invested in Anthropic in 2023.

NVIDIA was founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. The company is headquartered in Santa Clara, California. Jensen Huang serves as CEO of NVIDIA. NVIDIA released the H100 GPU in 2022, which became the dominant chip for training large language models. NVIDIA's CUDA platform is widely used for AI and machine learning workloads. NVIDIA competes with AMD and Intel in the semiconductor industry.

DeepMind was founded in 2010 by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa Suleyman. DeepMind is a subsidiary of Google, having been acquired in 2014. The lab is headquartered in London, United Kingdom. Demis Hassabis serves as CEO of DeepMind. DeepMind released AlphaFold in 2020, a groundbreaking system for predicting protein structures. DeepMind also developed AlphaGo, which defeated the world champion in the game of Go in 2016.

Amazon Web Services, known as AWS, is a subsidiary of Amazon. Amazon was founded in 1994 by Jeff Bezos and is headquartered in Seattle, Washington. Andy Jassy serves as CEO of Amazon. AWS invested in Anthropic in 2023, contributing up to four billion dollars to Anthropic's development. Amazon released Bedrock in 2023, a managed service for running foundation models including models from Anthropic.

Mistral AI was founded in 2023 by Arthur Mensch, Guillaume Lample, and Timothée Lacroix, all former researchers from DeepMind and Meta. The company is headquartered in Paris, France. Arthur Mensch serves as CEO of Mistral AI. Mistral AI released the Mistral 7B model in 2023, which gained attention for its strong performance relative to its small size. Microsoft invested in Mistral AI in 2024 through a partnership agreement.

xAI was founded in 2023 by Elon Musk. The company is headquartered in Palo Alto, California. Elon Musk serves as CEO of xAI. xAI released Grok, an AI assistant integrated with the X social media platform, in 2023. Elon Musk was a co-founder of OpenAI but departed from its board in 2018. xAI competes with OpenAI in the large language model market.

Cohere was founded in 2019 by Aidan Gomez, Ivan Zhang, and Nick Frosst. The company is headquartered in Toronto, Canada. Aidan Gomez serves as CEO of Cohere. Cohere focuses on providing large language model APIs and embedding models for enterprise customers. Cohere released the Command R series of models in 2024, optimized for retrieval-augmented generation tasks.

Stability AI was founded in 2019 by Emad Mostaque. The company is headquartered in London, United Kingdom. Stability AI released Stable Diffusion in 2022, an open-source text-to-image generation model that became widely adopted. Stability AI competes with Midjourney and DALL-E from OpenAI in the image generation market. DALL-E was released by OpenAI in 2021.

Hugging Face was founded in 2016 by Clément Delangue, Julien Chaumond, and Thomas Wolf. The company is headquartered in New York, United States. Hugging Face operates as a collaborative platform for machine learning models and datasets. Google invested in Hugging Face in 2023. Salesforce and Amazon also invested in Hugging Face. Hugging Face released the Transformers library, which became the most widely used open-source library for natural language processing.

Intel was founded in 1968 by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore. The company is headquartered in Santa Clara, California. Pat Gelsinger serves as CEO of Intel. Intel competes with NVIDIA and AMD in the semiconductor and AI chip markets. Intel acquired Habana Labs in 2019, a company that develops AI training chips. Intel released the Gaudi series of AI accelerators to compete with NVIDIA's H100 GPUs.

AMD, or Advanced Micro Devices, was founded in 1969 by Jerry Sanders. The company is headquartered in Santa Clara, California. Lisa Su serves as CEO of AMD. AMD released the MI300X GPU in 2023, targeting the AI training and inference market. AMD competes with NVIDIA in the GPU market and with Intel in the CPU market. Microsoft and Meta have both deployed AMD GPUs in their data centers.

Samsung Electronics is a subsidiary of Samsung Group, a South Korean conglomerate. Samsung is headquartered in Suwon, South Korea. Samsung produces semiconductors, including NAND flash memory and DRAM, which are critical components for AI servers. Samsung competes with TSMC and Intel in the semiconductor foundry market. Samsung acquired Harman International in 2017.

Baidu was founded in 2000 by Robin Li and Eric Xu. The company is headquartered in Beijing, China. Robin Li serves as CEO of Baidu. Baidu released ERNIE Bot, its large language model and AI assistant, in 2023. Baidu competes with Google in the search engine market and with OpenAI in the AI assistant market. Baidu is sometimes referred to as the Google of China.

Scale AI was founded in 2016 by Alexandr Wang. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California. Alexandr Wang serves as CEO of Scale AI. Scale AI provides data labeling and AI evaluation services to major technology companies including Microsoft and Meta. Microsoft invested in Scale AI. Scale AI competes with Appen and Labelbox in the AI data services market.

Palantir Technologies was founded in 2003 by Peter Thiel, Alex Karp, Joe Lonsdale, Stephen Cohen, and Nathan Gettings. The company is headquartered in Denver, Colorado. Alex Karp serves as CEO of Palantir. Palantir released AIP, its Artificial Intelligence Platform, in 2023 to help enterprises deploy AI and large language models on their private data. Palantir collaborates with Microsoft Azure for cloud deployments and with various defense and intelligence agencies as customers.
